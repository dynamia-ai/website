This weekend, the **Dynamia** team will head to Amsterdam to attend the upcoming [KubeCon + CloudNativeCon Europe 2026](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/kubecon-cloudnativecon-europe/). As one of the most influential conferences in the global cloud native space, this year's KubeCon is expected to attract over **13,000 developers, architects, and infrastructure practitioners** to discuss Kubernetes, AI, and next-generation infrastructure.

![KubeCon EU 2026 attracts 13,000 attendees](/images/blog/dynamia-and-hami-at-kubecon-eu-2026/kubecon-eu-2026.png)

As the initiator and leading contributor of the CNCF Sandbox project [HAMi](https://github.com/project-hami/hami), Dynamia will represent the HAMi community in multiple core activities during the conference, including Project Pavilion booth, official technical sessions, main stage Live Demo, and the post-conference AI Native Summit. This represents not only a concentrated project showcase but also a comprehensive presentation of HAMi in the global cloud native and AI infrastructure ecosystem.

## HAMi's Global Impact

HAMi is not a fledgling project; it is gradually becoming an important component of GPU virtualization and scheduling in the Kubernetes ecosystem.

Currently, HAMi has established preliminary influence globally:

* **Continuously growing GitHub Stars**, with increasing developer attention

* **Global user and contributor base**, covering 17 countries and regions including China, Europe, and North America, with over 500 contributors

* **Production deployments across multiple enterprise scenarios**, improving GPU utilization and multi-tenant resource management capabilities, including SF Technology, Beike, PREP EDU, DaoCloud, and others

* **Accepted into CNCF Sandbox and incubating**, becoming part of the cloud native AI infrastructure ecosystem

* **Gradual integration with Kubernetes ecosystem projects**, including scheduling and AI components like Volcano, Kueue, vLLM, and others

Starting from "solving GPU utilization problems," HAMi is evolving into a key infrastructure layer for AI workloads on Kubernetes.

## Why This KubeCon Matters

Viewing overall trends, a significant change this year is that AI is gradually moving from the application layer to the core position of the cloud native system. In past years, KubeCon discussions focused more on containers, microservices, and platform engineering. This year, whether in keynotes, AI-related sessions, or Project Pavilion project distribution, a clearer signal is being conveyed: **AI is becoming one of the main themes in the Kubernetes ecosystem**.

Meanwhile, the role of GPUs is also changing. It's no longer just an "acceleration device" on a single node, but is being redefined as a foundational resource that needs scheduling, sharing, and fine-grained management. GPU virtualization, resource utilization, multi-tenant isolation, and scheduling strategies are becoming key issues in AI infrastructure—and these are exactly what HAMi focuses on.

## Dynamia's Role at the Conference

At KubeCon Europe 2026, **Dynamia** will participate as the **HAMi community representative and project lead**.

In open source scenarios like Project Pavilion, we'll center on HAMi as a CNCF project, discussing GPU virtualization, sharing, and scheduling issues with developers and community members. This dual identity of "community + company" is an important characteristic of HAMi's current development stage.
